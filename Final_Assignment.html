STEP 3: Explore to what extend UNDP contributes to the global CO2 concentration?

Comments on the Data and Interacivity:

Within the next 3 steps you are able to explore your own, your office's and all the other UNDP Offices' Carbon Footprint according to the Wh consumption. Please feel free to test this pilot version and get a feeling of how energy is consumed. Maybe you are even able to identify energy saving potentials. We will start on an aggregeated level - STEP 3 - and drill down on the data over circuit level - STEP 4 - to device level STEP 5. All the data visualized is hourly time series energy consumption data, measured with SmartMeters within UNDP Country Offices. On the right side of the interactive visualization idioms you will always find filtering options and an overview on the data availibility. This helps interpreting the data since some visual conspicuities are caused due to wrong measurements approaches or NA values. On the right hand site you will always find the CLIMATE ACTION Boxes, offering you hints on what to explorer within the underlying data set.

Visual Encodings

  • Size of the circles: Number of UNDP Staff
  • Color Hue: Overall consumption of the Country Office relative to each other.

Potential Results Perfomed in STEP 5:

Next Step - Interpretation Findings + CLIMATE ACTION BOX

In the STEP 3, we identified Mauritania is one of the inefficient Country Offices. In STEP 4, we found that, apart from the AC's the 1st floor is consuming a lot. Drilling down to the 1st floor circuits in STEP 5, we can see a very high base load for IT Equipment. According to literature and interviews in the UNDP environment, the high base load could be caused by devices switched on but not needed. Check out the CLINATE ACTION box, and learn how how can reduce your base load consumption with just some little interventions. Studies showed that these interventions can lead to a reduction up to 20% to 30%. Try it and explore on this page, how much energy you saved.
